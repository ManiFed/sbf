When FTX collapsed in November 2022, the immediate narrative was one of fraud,hubris and misappropriated customer funds. Two years on, a second story is emergingfrom the wreckage; one about investment timing that stands out in the history ofventure-backed crypto portfolios. A reconstruction of Sam Bankman-Fried’s pre-collapse positions across six assets shows a portfolio that, had it been held intact, wouldhave grown from roughly $4.7 billion to an estimated $52.5 billion; a paper gain of$47.8 billion.The analysis, first circulated on X by crypto analyst Axel Bitblaze (@Axel_bitblaze69) onFebruary 14, 2026, drew renewed attention after Anthropic’s latest fundraising roundvaulted the AI company’s valuation to $380 billion, according to an official Anthropicpress release. The positions span artificial intelligence, consumer finance, blockchaininfrastructure, Bitcoin mining, and private space. These sectors collectively dominatedthe investment landscape of the 2024 to 2026 bull cycle.
The Anthropic Stake: The Most Expensive Early Exit In AIHistoryFTX invested $500 million in Anthropic in 2021, acquiring an 8% stake in the then-nascent AI safety company. According to a Reuters report from February 2024, thebankruptcy estate sold that stake for $1.3 billion, representing a reasonable outcome atthe time, given prevailing AI valuations. At Anthropic's current $380 billion valuation,the same 8% position would be worth approximately $30.4 billion. The estate realizedless than 5% of what that stake would eventually be worth, selling into a market that wasstill pricing AI startups conservatively, roughly twelve months before the sector reset itsceiling.As Blocknow reported, the sale accelerated FTX's bankruptcy repayment process, but itlocked in a loss of more than $29 billion in potential value relative to today's mark. FTXcreditors were paid at 2024 prices. The assets were worth 2026 prices.

The estate sold at a fraction of that,constrained by lock-up schedules and the urgency of bankruptcy proceedings. Robinhood: $648 Million In At $11.52, Worth $5.7 Billion TodayIn May 2022, Sam Bankman-Fried acquired a 7.6% stake in Robinhood through hisholding company Emergent Fidelity Technologies, paying $648 million at an averageprice of $11.52 per share, per a filing with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission.The purchase made Bankman-Fried the third-largest Robinhood shareholder at thetime. With Robinhood's market capitalization now approaching $75 billion, that 7.6%position would be valued at approximately $5.7 billion, a nearly ninefold return on theinitial outlay. The shares were seized by the U.S. Department of Justice following FTX'scollapse and ultimately returned to the bankruptcy estate.K5 Global and SpaceX: A $700 Million Bet on AccessBankman-Fried directed Alameda Research to transfer $700 million to K5 Global andaffiliated entities in 2022. K5, co-founded by Hollywood agent and political operativeMichael Kives, holds stakes in SpaceX, Anduril, Airbnb, and xAI. FTX's bankruptcyestate subsequently sued K5 to claw back the funds, describing the investment as madewithout due diligence, according to court filings cited by PYMNTS. The dispute wasresolved in January 2025, when FTX reached a settlement retaining its stake in K5'sfunds, which FTX CEO John Ray described as a 'bright spot' in the recovery portfolio.Given SpaceX's private market valuation now exceeding $350 billion, the $700 millionexposure via K5 carries a notional present value of approximately $3 billion.Genesis Digital Assets: $1.15 Billion Into Bitcoin MiningBetween August 2021 and April 2022, Alameda Research invested $1.15 billion acrossfour rounds in Genesis Digital Assets, a Bitcoin miner then operating primarily inKazakhstan. The FTX Recovery Trust subsequently sued GDA in September 2025,calling the investment one of Bankman-Fried’s most reckless, citing overpaid valuationsand unaudited financials that the trust said bore no relation to reality. Genesis Digitalhas since moved its operations to Dubai and has explored a U.S. IPO. Its currentvaluation is estimated at approximately $3.5 billion, meaning the original $1.15 billioninvestment would carry a 3x unrealized return today; modest by the standards of therest of the portfolio, but still a positive outcome on paper.Sui: $100 Million In at Series B, Worth $1.2 Billion At PeakFTX Ventures led a $300 million Series B in Mysten Labs, the developer behind the Suiblockchain, in 2022, pushing Sui's implied valuation above $2 billion. The bankruptcyestate subsequently sold its SUI token warrants and preferred stock for less than $100million, according to a court document filed March 22, 2023. Bankman-Fried was sentenced to 25 years in prison for fraud andmisappropriation of customer funds. The investments described above were made usingcommingled deposits, not proprietary capital. Most were flagged by the FTX RecoveryTrust as potentially fraudulent transfers. None of this changes the underlying assetselection quality.As documented in bankruptcy proceedings cited by Coin Bureau, FTX’s problem wasstructural: long-duration illiquid positions were held against short-duration customerdeposit liabilities. When withdrawal requests surged to $6 billion in 48 hours followingCoinDesk’s publication of Alameda’s balance sheet in November 2022, positions thatcould not be liquidated quickly; an 8% stake in Anthropic cannot be sold in an afternoon(at least not then, very possible it would be sold immediately today). The assets were realand the bets excellent in hidsight, but the governance and management of funds wasn’t.The bankruptcy estate ultimately recovered approximately $18 billion for creditors,enabling repayments of 118% to 143% of petition-date claims, according to FTX CEOJohn Ray’s estimates. Had the portfolio been held intact to today, the notional valuewould have been approximately $52.5 billion , nearly three times what was recovered,and more than enough to pay every creditor in full with a substantial surplus remaining.
